Project I: Courses Performance and Revenue Report

Analysis report on the revenues generated by and the performance of web development, graphic design, musical instruments and business finance courses on Udemy.

PROJECTS

Project I: Courses Performance and Revenue Report

August 15, 2021

Business Problem

You’re a Data Analyst working for the education tech company Udemy. You have been asked by your manager, Head of Curriculum at Udemy, to present the data on course revenue, and you have been provided with data on courses from different topics to understand where opportunities to increase revenue may lie, and track the performance of courses. Your manager has suggested encouraging Web Development courses to charge more because she believes that these are the most popular courses. She needs to send a report to the CEO in the next three weeks on how they will increase their next quarterly earnings.

To understand the performance of web development, graphic design, business finance and musical instrument courses and to see where the opportunities for generating more revenue lie, I performed the following analysis and was able to discover the best performing and most revenue generating subject through visualizations and came to a data driven conclusion on the best way to increase revenue for Udemy.

DATA INFORMATION

The datasets contained information such as course id, course title, course url, price, number of subscribers, number of reviews, number of lectures, levels, rating, content duration, published date, published time, subject, subscription type and revenue generated. A total of 3672 courses from the year 2011-2017 were used for this analysis.

DESIGN

Data Cleaning

The data on the different courses came as separate csv files. They were then merged together on a single spreadsheet. As a result, there were some inconsistent formatting, trailing whitespaces, blank cells and duplicate data. To clean the data, I performed the following:

  • Removed duplicate data using the remove duplicate function
  • Removed blank cells from the data using filter by condition
  • All columns were ensured to be in the right format
  • Trailing whitespaces were removed using Trim function
  • Find and replace function was used to make the wed development subject inputs consistent with other subjects

DATA VISUALIZATION

Excel and Tableau were used to create pivot tables and charts respectively.

FINDINGS 1: REVENUE ANALYSIS

The following insights were drawn from the analysis

The Top 20 Most Subscribed Courses

17 out of the top 20 most subscribed courses are web development courses, 2 musical instrument courses and 1 business finance course.

Went further to see how many of these courses generated revenue (paid courses)

It turned out that 12 out of the top 20 most subscribed courses are free courses while 8 are paid courses

Next, I proceeded to check the top 20 revenue generating courses.

The Top 20 Revenue Generating Courses

The pivot table below shows the top 20 revenue generating courses on Udemy

18 out of the top 20 revenue generating courses are web development courses, 1 musical instrument course and 1 design course. The course that generated the highest revenue of $24,316,800.00 was the web developer bootcamp course followed by the complete web developer course 2.0 that generated a revenue of $22,902,400.00.

Next, I checked to see how many of the top 20 most subscribed courses were in the top 20 revenue generating courses. I found out using Vlookup function that only 7 courses out of the top 20 most subscribed courses were in the top 20 revenue generating courses category; 6 of which are web development courses and 1 was a musical instrument course.

Web development courses outperformed other courses in revenue generation and also had more online engagements based on the number of subscribers. Opportunities for generating more revenue lie in web development courses.
