INTRODUCTION

This is a project done in partial fulfillment of the requirement for earning a certificate of completion in the google data analytics course.This project covers the various steps of the data analysis process such as Ask, Prepare, Process, Analyze, Share and Act.

Scenario

You are a junior data analyst working in the marketing analyst team at Cyclistic, a bike-share company in Chicago. The director of marketing believes the company’s future success depends on maximizing the number of annual memberships. Therefore, your team wants to understand how casual riders and annual members use Cyclistic bikes differently. From these insights, your team will design a new marketing strategy to convert casual riders into annual members. But first, Cyclistic executives must approve your recommendations, so they must be backed up with compelling data insights and professional data visualization

BUSINESS TASK

To understand how annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently and to see how casual riders can become annual members through digital media influence

DATA SOURCE

The data used for this analysis is stored in a public cloud storage and organized by date. Each dataset was contained in a zip file and most of the files were in csv format.

Data Integrity: The data is reliable. It is a first party data made available by a bicycle sharing company called Divvy under Motivate International Inc. license for public use. It contained all information needed to find answers to the question being asked. It also contained both historical and current data that are relevant to the business task at hand.

The information contained in the data are as follows:

Column Description
Ride_id: ID attached to each ride taken
Rideable_type: Type of bicycle ridden ( Electric bike, Docked bike, Classic bike)
Started_at: Day and time ride started
Ended_at: Day and time ride ended
Start_station_name: Name of station where ride originated
Start_station_id: ID of station where trip originated
End_station_name: Name of station where trip terminated
End_station_id: ID of station where trip terminated
Start_lat: Start station latitude
Start_lng: Start station longitude
End_lat: End station latitude
End_lng: End station longitude
member-casual: “member” a rider who purchased annual membership; “casual” a rider who purchased a single ride or full day pass.

DATA CLEANING AND MANIPULATION

  • Checked the structure of each of the files to see if they are compatible and can be merged into a single dataframe
  • Compared the column names of each Of the files to ensure they matched perfectly before joining them into a single dataframe.
  • Converted start_station_id and end_station_id to character so that they can stack correctly
  • Removed unwanted columns such start_lat, start_lng, end_lat and end_lng
  • The data could only be aggregated at the ride-level, which is too granular. So, I added some additional columns of data such as day, month, year that provide additional opportunities to aggregate the data.
  • Added a calculated field for length of ride which was done by subtracting start time of each trip from the end time of each trip
  • Removed all rows with negative ride length values which could be as a result of the periods when some bikes were taken out of circulation for quality control reasons.

DESCRIPTIVE ANALYSIS

  • Calculated the average ride length, median ride length, the maximum and minimum ride lengths over a 12 month period (July 2020 to July 2021)
  • Calculated the average ride length, median ride length, the maximum and minimum ride lengths for each rider i.e casual riders and annual members to see how they ride differently
  • The average ride time by each day of the week for members vs casual users was also calculated.

FINDINGS

The following key findings were made from the visualizations

  • Casual riders account for the longest rides records in Cyclistic. They ride for longer period of time than annual members
  • Casual riders ride mostly on weekends(Saturdays and Sundays) while annual members ride mostly during the week. Figure 2 shows that annual members ride more bikes from Monday till Friday than casual riders.

RECOMMENDATIONS

I will recommend that Cyclistic makes the cost of being an annual member relatively cheaper to that of casual riders in order to attract casual riders into becoming annual members. This can be acheived through digital media where the company gets to advertise promotional offers for their existing members and those who will sign up for annual membership.
